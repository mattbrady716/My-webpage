Learning to Learn

Programmers approach problems in a very methodical way that allows them to tackle complex problems. There are three steps to this process. First, the stage must be set. This means to clear distractions, establish what is to be learned, and to prioritize process over product. Second, is to play with the problem. This means wrestling with it finding what works, taking risks, and having fun solving the puzzle. Finally, the last step is to reflect on the process. This means self-assessment by asking questions of what went well and what didn't work. It also means learning from the experience and repeating these steps. Goal Setting

A dream is something you wish would happen; a goal is something you intend to make happen. Setting SMART goals is an excellent way to accomplish more. SMART is an acronym that stands for specific, measurable, attainable, relevant, and time-bound. These are pretty self-explanatory. It basically means to state exactly what you are trying to learn over the course of a specific time period. That goal should be relevant to what you are trying to learn and be attainable. Also, the goal should be set in a way that it is easily seen whether the goal is met or not. I have included links to a few reflections of mine on goal setting below.
