Meet Up Info

GatsbyJS and Reason with ReasonReact meetup on Tuesday February 20,2018

People I Met

  • Danny Radden who was a React Developer for 303 Software in CO. He graduated the Turing school fairly recently.
  • Josh Long who was a React event cordinator. He was also running this event as well.

What I Learned

This was my first experience with React. I had no idea what it was before the meeting. I learned that the GatsbyJS language deals with the user-interface. It has many pros including server side rendering. The presenter had built a wordpress page that showed how changes can almost instantly be seen on the browser. The page wasn't reloading anytime when navigating internally throughout the site. He said it was a bit difficult to learn, but once learned it was simple to use. It was open sourced and frequently updated by developers. Overall, it seemed like a really cool thing to use for simplistic projects, but it had it's limitations.

Meet Up Info

New Tech Meetup Denver March 15, 2018

People I Met

  • Jeff Cozart CEO of Juniper Unmanned
  • Taylor Kaplan CEO of Centari, a software rescue consulting company

What I Learned

I really enjoyed this meet up. It was very organized. They had a speaker and had a lot of job opportunities along with good networking. The speaker was Jeff Cozart who was the CEO of Juniper Unmanned. As the CEO he definitely was coming from the business side of things, but still had a lot of interesting stuff to share. Some of their latest projects used drones and helicopters to predict future potholes on roads, observe different components along utility lines, and calculate how grades on roads affected fuel economy of construction equipment.

During the Q&A portion of the meet up a lot of the attendees were asking questions about security in terms of location services on phones. I found it interesting how many ways they could pinpoint you even without those services turned on. A couple of the ones mentioned were air pressure, time zone, altitude, cell phone towers, and a couple apps like Uber were mentioned. We then went into a discussion on the pros and cons of everything becoming AI. It was cool to see all the different perspectives coming from different backrounds.

Questions I Have

  • As I mentioned before the speaker was coming from the business side of things and wasn't super technical. He seemed to really focus on the Financial stuff. I found his topic kind of interesting but, I would like to dive a little further into it or hear from someone on the more tech side of things.
